Image: Mount Fuji
Japan's Iconic Peak

Mount Fuji

Standing at 3,776 meters, Mount Fuji is Japan's highest peak and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The climbing season runs from July to September.

Image: Fushimi Inari
Thousands of Torii Gates

Fushimi Inari Shrine

Located in southern Kyoto, this shrine is famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates that wind through the forested mountainside.

Image: Shibuya Crossing
World's Busiest Intersection

Shibuya Crossing

Known as the world's busiest pedestrian crossing, Shibuya Crossing is a symbol of Tokyo's vibrant urban energy and modern culture.

Image: Hiroshima Memorial
A Symbol of Peace

Hiroshima Peace Memorial

The Atomic Bomb Dome stands as a powerful reminder of the devastation of nuclear weapons and a symbol of hope for world peace.

Image: Nara Park
Friendly Wild Deer

Nara Park

Home to over 1,000 freely roaming deer, Nara Park is one of Japan's most beloved natural attractions and a must-visit destination.

Image: Bamboo Grove
Enchanting Bamboo Forest

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

Walk through towering bamboo stalks that create a magical, otherworldly atmosphere. One of Kyoto's most photographed locations.
