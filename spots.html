Japan's Iconic Peak

Mount Fuji

Standing at 3,776 meters, Mount Fuji is Japan's highest and most sacred mountain. This UNESCO World Heritage Site offers breathtaking views and unforgettable climbing experiences during summer months.

Ancient Temple of Pure Water

Kiyomizu-dera Temple

One of Kyoto's most celebrated temples, Kiyomizu-dera stands on wooden pillars overlooking the city. The temple's spring water is believed to bring health, longevity, and success in studies.

World's Busiest Intersection

Shibuya Scramble Crossing

Experience Tokyo's electric energy at the world's busiest pedestrian crossing. Up to 3,000 people cross simultaneously, creating an iconic symbol of modern Japan's vibrant urban culture.

Tropical Paradise of Japan

Okinawa

Discover Japan's tropical paradise with crystal-clear waters, pristine beaches, and vibrant coral reefs. Okinawa offers a unique blend of Japanese and Ryukyuan culture with warm hospitality year-round.

Nostalgic Nightlife Hub

Shinjuku Golden Gai

Step into Tokyo's nostalgic nightlife district with over 200 tiny bars packed into narrow alleyways. Each establishment offers a unique atmosphere and intimate conversations with locals and travelers alike.

Spectacular Summer Festival

Aomori Nebuta Festival

Witness one of Japan's most dynamic summer festivals featuring enormous illuminated floats depicting mythical warriors and gods. The powerful music and energetic dancers create an unforgettable spectacle every August.
